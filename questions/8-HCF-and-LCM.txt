HCF and LCM:
------------

1) Find the LCM of 4, 6 and 8.

2) 

a) Find the first 5 multiples of 9 and the first 5 multiples of 12.
b) Hence find the LCM of 9 and 12.

3) Find the LCM of each of the following pairs of numbers.

a) 3 and 4
b) 2 and 10
c) 10 and 15
d) 15 and 20 

4) Find the LCM of each of the sets of numbers below.

a) 3, 6, 8
b) 3, 5, 6
c) 4, 9, 12

5) Mike visits Oscar every 4 days, while Narinda visits Oscar every 5 days. If they both
visted today, how many days will it be before they visit on the same day again?

6) A garden centre has between 95 and 205 ptted plants. They can be arranged exactly in rows
of 25 and exactly in rows of 30. How many plants are there?

7) Find the HCF of 12 and 15.

8) 

a) Find the common factors of 12 and 20.
b) Hence find the HCF of 12 and 20.

9) Write down the following:

a) the HCF of 11 and 12
b) the HCF of 21 and 22

10) Find the HCF of the following sets of numbers?

a) 6, 8, 16
b) 12, 15, 18
c) 18, 36, 72

11) Find the HCF of 48 and 60.

12) Write 380 as a product of its prime factors.
